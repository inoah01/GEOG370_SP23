Projection #1: Cylindrical Equal Area

For my first projection, I decided to use the Cylindrical Equal Area projection
According to ArcGIS, this rectangular projection originates from a Swiss mathematician named
John H. Lambert and was first mentioned in 1772. Looking at the projection with
Tissot's indicatrix applied, we can see that this projection does a pretty decent
job at preserving space, direction, and area, but distorts the shape pretty badly
as you approach the +/- 30-90* latitudes. This increasing distortion towards the
poles supports the claim on ArcGIS that this projection is best used for
large-scale maps closer to the equator.

Projection #2: Aitoff

For my second projection, I decided to use the Aitoff projection. ArcGIS mentions
the Aitoff projection is a "modified azimuthal projection". The ArcGIS article also
mentions that this projection was "developed by Russian cartographer David A. Aitoff
in 1889." Tissot's indicatrix demonstrates that this projection distorts shape, area,
and direction to some extent. Most obviously, the projection distorts the direction
and area as you deviate away from the Prime Meridian. Based on this, it would seem that
this projection is used for much smaller-scale maps than the Cylindrical Equal Area projection
and the Gall Stereographic projection.

Projection #3: Gall Stereographic

For my third and final projection, I decided to use the Gall Stereographic Projection. The
Gall Stereographic projection is another rectangular projection and according to ArcGIS, was
created by James Gall in 1855. With Tissot's indicatrix, we can see that the distortion of the Gall
Stereographic projection is similar to that of the Cylindrical Equal Area projection. Since they are both
types of cylindrical projections, they both experience the same increasing distortion as you approach
the poles. The biggest difference between the two projections is that it appears the Gall Stereographic
does a better job preserving shape as you approach the poles. Like the Cylindrical Equal Area projection,
this projection would be best for large-scale maps with a focus closer to the equator than the poles.
