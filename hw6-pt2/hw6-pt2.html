Homework 6 Part 2

Below are my three choropleth maps of Michigan counties looking at the median household income
to median with cash rent ratio. In order to calculate this ratio, I first had to revisit my data from
homework 6 part 1 and get the column for household income as opposed to family household income. The other
key part of this ratio was rental data. I obtained this data through the same census data source as the
median income data. After following a similar procedure for cleaning the data, I joined the two new columns to
the Michigan counties vector layer that I had made for homework 6 part 1.

I chose median household income to median with cash rent ratio to look at relative housing affordability in
Michigan counties. Though the method of classification varies between the maps below, one fact
remains constant, a higher ration indicates a higher cost of living. What makes further interpreting these
results difficult, is correctly choosing the right classification method. Some ratios were hardly above 2,
while others were greater than 10. This variation in values means that choosing an inappropriate classification
method could result in missed outliers and patterns, or in the context of the ratio, incorrectly assuming interpreting
the cost of living of a particular county. The three different classification modes I chose to compare are Equal Counts,
Logarithmic Scale, and Pretty Breaks. Please click on the respective map for more information on the classification mode.
